- 2026-06-16 ŌĆö **Send-side socket-buffer bloat fixed at the right layer (`TxPacer`); rig retest pending.** Headset QA exposed the real landmine #1. The native producer queue is capped + drops-to-latest, but that cap is *upstream* of the L2CAP socket; reliable CoC delivery means the kernel/BlueDroid TX FIFO absorbs the (50 fps ŌłÆ link-drain) deficit, and under classic-SCO headset coexistence the BLE ACL link drains far below 50 fps, so the FIFO fills to seconds and every later frame plays behind the backlog (rig logs `artifacts/logs/*-074111`: Pixel txSeqŌēł2200 vs moto rxSeqŌēł600 ŌåÆ ~1600 frames Ōēł 32 s in flight; native `qDepth` stayed 0 throughout ŌĆö the metric watched the wrong queue). The two prior native patches (drop-to-latest, jitter trim) sat upstream of the socket and could not move it. Fix: `TxPacer` (pure, clock-injected, unit-tested) wall-clock-paces socket writes with freshest-wins drop and never bursts to catch up after a stall; AIMD backoff keyed on `write()` stall (the only locally observable congestion signal) converges the offered rate toward link drain; base rate drops to 25 fps on a radio-sharing BT route (`VoiceAudioRoute.isCommunicationRouteDegraded`). New Kotlin-layer `TXPACE` DIAG (offered/sent/paceDropped/stalls/intervalMs) makes socket pressure visible at last. Local validation passed: ktlint, detekt, `testDebugUnitTest` (9 new `TxPacer` tests), `assembleDebug`. Installed the debug APK on Pixel 10 Pro XL + moto g play 2024. **Audio retest pending** ŌĆö this bounds latency (real-time-or-drop) rather than guaranteeing intelligible audio under SCO; if `TXPACE`/native DIAG show sustained `sent` Ōē½ delivered, the headset path also needs true 40 ms packetization (bundle 2 frames/packet) and/or LE Audio.
